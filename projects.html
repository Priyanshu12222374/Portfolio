Vision-Based Tree Health Monitoring

Objective: This study contributes to the field of environmental conservation by developing an automated system for monitoring tree health in forests using drone imagery and computer vision.

Outcome: Achieved a classification accuracy of 93.2%, demonstrating the practical effectiveness of drone-based computer vision systems for large-scale forest health assessment and enabling timely intervention by forest management authorities.

Key Insights: Designed a complete pipeline involving high-resolution UAV-based data collection, semantic segmentation for tree crown isolation, and deep learning-based classification to distinguish healthy, diseased, and dead trees.

Tech Stack: Python, TensorFlow, OpenCV, DJI Drone Systems, ResNet-50 CNN Model.
